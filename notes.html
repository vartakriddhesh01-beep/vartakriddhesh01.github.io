Finance Notes & Resources

Valuation principles, corporate finance concepts, and investment philosophy inspired by Aswath Damodaran

Valuation Principles

Intrinsic Valuation

  • Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) methodology
  • Free Cash Flow to Firm (FCFF) vs Free Cash Flow to Equity (FCFE)
  • Terminal value calculations: Perpetuity growth vs Exit multiples
  • Cost of capital: WACC, Cost of equity, Cost of debt

Relative Valuation

  • Comparable company analysis
  • Precedent transactions
  • Valuation multiples: P/E, EV/EBITDA, P/B, P/S
  • Normalizing adjustments and controlling premiums

Corporate Finance

Capital Structure

  • Optimal debt-equity mix
  • Trade-off theory: Tax benefits vs Bankruptcy costs
  • Pecking order theory
  • Leveraged buyouts (LBOs) and recapitalizations

Capital Budgeting

  • Net Present Value (NPV)
  • Internal Rate of Return (IRR)
  • Payback period and discounted payback
  • Real options in investment decisions

Investment Philosophy

Damodaran's Framework

  • Narrative-driven investing
  • Margin of safety concept
  • Process over outcome
  • Long-term value creation

Risk Assessment

  • Beyond beta: Business risk, financial risk
  • Country risk and currency risk
  • Narrative risk and storytelling
  • Scenario analysis and stress testing

"The essence of valuation is storytelling with numbers. A good valuation is a narrative that is grounded in numbers, and a good narrative needs numbers to give it discipline." — Aswath Damodaran
